Accretion in Galactic Centres

Both Sgr A* (the nearest supermassive black hole at the centre of our own Milky Way) and M87* (the SMBH lurking in the depths of one of the most massive galaxies in the local universe) are the targets of serveral groundbreaking experiments testing gravitational and plasma physics theories. Multiwavelength coordination is key to constraining the radiative mechanisms at play during flaring events.

Sagittarius A*'s Variability

Sgr A* is variable at all wavelengths that it is observed. From radio, to sub-mm, to NIR, to X-rays, the black hole at our galaxy's center brightens and dims on timescales from minutes to weeks. "Flickering" in the NIR, occasionally (~1/day) a sudden increase of flux will be observed, often (but not always) accompanied by a distict X-ray flare.

CFHT (Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope)

An optical telescope located atop the summit of Maunakea, a 4200 meter, dormant volcano located on the islands of the kama'aina of Hawai'i (people of the land). It is one of several telescopes on sacred āina (land) protected by the Historical Preservation Act for its significance to Hawaiian culture. The University of Hawai'i manages the site and the State of Hawaiʻi's Department of Land and Natural Resources (DLNR) leases the land to multi-national facilities like the CFHT. The lease will expire in 2033. See this timeline for a history of astronomy on Maunakea.

The observatory headquarters is located in the country-town of Waimea (population of 13,000). The facility is primarily funded by the National Research Council of Canada, Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique of France, and the University of Hawaii .
