Hope Boyce

black holes | galactic centres | Sgr A* | gas kinematics

I use space- and ground-based telescopes to study the relationship between supermassive black holes, the material accreting around them, and the centres of galaxies. I recently finished my PhD at the McGill Space Institute working with Prof. Daryl Haggard in Tiohtià:ke (Montréal, QC).

Sagittarius A*'s Variability

I use coordinated multiwavelength observations of the black hole at the center of our galaxy to constrain the emission mechanisms of flares originating in the accretion flow near the event horizon. Learn More

Multiwavelength coordination with the Event Horizon Telescope

Multiwavelength observations characterize the broad band SED and variability at the time of EHT's imaging of the black hole's shadow and accretion flow. Learn More

Measuring BH masses with Molecular Gas

We model the gravitational potential of galactic centres using known stellar distributions (from Hubble) and high-resolution kinematics of molecular gas (from ALMA) to constrain the presence and mass of SMBHs.Learn More

What Causes Star Formation Suppression in the Centres of Galaxies?

Star formation efficiency (SFE) is observed to be anomalously low in the bulges & centres of galaxies. We target these environments and combine tracers of star formation with measurements of molecular gas to examine SFE with corellated physical measures at resolutions of ~100pc. Learn More

Searching for an Intermediate Mass Black Hole

Created a large scale stellar velocity map of the Large Magellenic Cloud with MUSE data to constrain the kinematic center and pave the way for a search for an IMBH. Learn More
